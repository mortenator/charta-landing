ThinkCell costs $800 a year and only runs in PowerPoint. For firms standardized on Microsoft Office, that's a defensible spend. For anyone building decks in Google Slides, it doesn't run at all.

That gap matters more than it used to. Google Workspace has 3 billion users. A growing share of consulting work, strategy decks, and investor reporting happens in Slides. ThinkCell hasn't shipped a Google integration in 20+ years of existence.

So the real question isn't "what's cheaper than ThinkCell?" It depends on where you work.

---

## PowerPoint: cheaper substitutes that actually work

If you're locked into PowerPoint and want to cut the $800/yr license, 3 tools are worth knowing.

**Mekko Graphics** is built by ex-ThinkCell people. Same chart types (waterfall, mekko, bridge, bar), similar workflow, lower price at $299/yr. Best direct substitute if you're already comfortable in the ThinkCell paradigm.

**Vizzlo** is browser-based with a PowerPoint plugin. Good for occasional charts, weaker on complex financial visuals. $18-35/month depending on plan.

**Datylon** produces the cleanest static charts of the group — closer to Illustrator than ThinkCell. More manual, higher quality ceiling, better for polished reports than live client decks. $180-360/yr.

None have AI. You're still entering data manually and adjusting inputs. You're paying less but the workflow is the same.

## Google Slides: the gap ThinkCell never filled

Google Sheets has native waterfall charts you can paste into Slides. The problem is the output: default colors, no connector lines, no bar labels. Fine for internal use, not for client decks.

The deeper problem is revision. Every time a number changes, you go back to Sheets, update the chart, re-paste it. The chart isn't live in your slide.

[Charta](https://getcharta.ai) is a Google Slides sidebar that generates charts from plain English. Describe what you want, get a formatted chart directly in your slide. Waterfall, bridge, mekko, bar, line, pie, gantt, scatter. The output is editable. When numbers change, regenerate with a new prompt.

The workflow for a Q4 revenue bridge:

```
Waterfall showing Q4 revenue bridge:
Starting revenue: $4.2M
New logos: +$800K
Expansion: +$320K
Churn: -$410K
Ending revenue: $4.91M
```

That generates a chart with correct bar colors, connector lines, and axis labels in under 60 seconds.

Free tier: 5 charts/day. Plus is $9/month for 20 charts/day. Business is $29/month unlimited.

## Developers: programmatic chart generation

One area where none of the legacy tools compete: API access.

Charta publishes an MCP server (`npx -y @charta/mcp`) that lets AI agents generate charts without a browser or human in the loop. If you're building a pipeline that produces reports, financial models, or executive summaries, chart generation becomes a single function call.

ThinkCell, Mekko Graphics, and Vizzlo have no API. Charta's entire surface is designed to be called from code.

## What about Canva, Beautiful.ai, and Gamma?

Canva is a design tool, not a chart tool. Beautiful.ai and Gamma generate full slide decks from AI prompts. If you want a deck built from scratch, they're reasonable. If you need a specific chart type dropped into an existing client presentation, none of them do that well.

The chart-specific niche is narrower and more demanding than general presentation AI. That's exactly why ThinkCell has dominated it for 20 years.
